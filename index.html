Specimen α — Primary Culture

DNA Methylation Actively Silences Genes, Century-Old Debate Settled

Source: Science Daily
Researchers at UNSW Sydney used a new CRISPR epigenetic editing technique to definitively prove that DNA methylation directly causes gene silencing, not merely correlates with it. Published in Nature Communications in January 2025, the study removed methyl groups from silenced fetal hemoglobin genes and watched them activate, then added the groups back and watched them shut down again. This resolves a decades-long scientific controversy and demonstrates a safer gene therapy approach that doesn't cut DNA. The technique could offer treatments for sickle cell disease without the unintended mutations that traditional CRISPR editing risks.

Chinese Immune Cell Atlas Challenges European-Centric Medical Research

Source: Nature
Published in Science in January 2026, the Chinese Immune Multi-Omics Atlas (CIMA) maps immune cell function across genes, proteins, RNA, and the epigenome in a Chinese cohort. The study reveals crucial differences from European and Japanese datasets that would be missed in European-centric studies. Per the research, these population-specific variations in immune cell function could affect drug responses, vaccine efficacy, and disease susceptibility. The atlas represents a massive functional mapping effort that complements existing datasets but shows why precision medicine requires population-diverse data.

The ruling resolves a circuit split and determines that such state requirements conflict with Federal Rules of Civil Procedure 8 and 12. Per the January 20, 2026 decision in Berk v.
