On January 13, 2026, the Supreme Court will hear arguments in two cases—Little v. Hecox and West Virginia v. B.P.J.—considering the extent to which gender identity and biological assignment at birth can be considered as factors in scholastic sports competitions. Idaho's Fairness in Women's Sports Act bases competition on biological assignment at birth, with the Ninth Circuit ruling the law violated 14th Amendment Equal Protection rights. In West Virginia, the Fourth Circuit overturned a district court ruling in favor of the state on Equal Protection and Title IX grounds. Ninth Circuit Reverses California Urban Open-Carry Ban in Second Amendment Case Ninth Circuit

The Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals issued a significant ruling on January 2, 2026, in Baird v. Bonta, reversing in part a district court's summary judgment upholding California's urban open-carry ban. The panel held that California law strictly prohibits the issuance of open-carry permits in areas where 95% of Californians live, affecting 30 counties with populations below 200,000. The court found that the overwhelming historical consensus that open carry was protected at both the Founding (1791) and during Reconstruction (1868) makes bans on open carry unconstitutional under the Second Amendment. The decision explicitly rejected California's argument that concealed carry permits can substitute for open carry rights.

FDA PDUFA Date Today: Sparsentan for Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis (FSGS) HCP Live

The FDA has a PDUFA action date of January 13, 2026, for sparsentan (Filspari) for the treatment of focal segmental glomerulosclerosis (FSGS). The application is supported by data from phase 3 DUPLEX and phase 2 DUET studies showing significant reduction in proteinuria compared to irbesartan in children and adults with FSGS. If approved, sparsentan would be the first treatment for this rare kidney disease, which is a leading cause of end-stage renal disease. The approval would establish proteinuria reduction as a validated regulatory endpoint in FSGS and provide a non-immunosuppressive, oral treatment option.

Pfizer Announces Positive Phase 3 Results for Hemophilia A Gene Therapy Pfizer

Pfizer announced positive topline results from the Phase 3 AFFINE study evaluating giroctocogene fitelparvovec, an investigational gene therapy for the treatment of adults with moderately severe to severe hemophilia A. The study achieved its primary objective of non-inferiority and superiority, with a statistically significant reduction in mean total annualized bleeding rate compared to the pre-infusion period (1.24 vs 4.73; one-sided p-value=0.0040). Following a single dose, the therapy demonstrated superior bleed protection compared with routine Factor VIII replacement prophylaxis treatment. Treatment-related adverse events generally resolved with clinical management. The results demonstrate the transformative potential of this gene therapy candidate to provide superior bleed protection while helping relieve treatment burden.
