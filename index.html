Iran Protests Death Toll Reaches 2,000 as Economic Crisis Deepens

About 2,000 people have been killed in ongoing protests in Iran according to an Iranian official who told Reuters the deaths included both protesters and security personnel. The official attributed the deaths to terrorists but provided no breakdown of casualties. U.S.-based Human Rights Activist News Agency reported that 10,721 people had been arrested and identified hundreds of those killed in the anti-government protests. Iran has eased some restrictions, allowing mobile phone calls abroad, but has not restored internet access or texting services. The protests have been driven by economic crisis linked to U.S. sanctions.

Supreme Court Hears Louisiana Oil Case That Could Reshape State vs. Federal Jurisdiction

Read more
The Supreme Court heard oral arguments Monday in Chevron USA v. Plaquemines Parish, a case that could determine whether oil companies can move state environmental lawsuits to friendlier federal courts. At stake is a Louisiana state jury verdict ordering Chevron to pay $745 million for wetlands restoration. The case centers on a World War II-era statute and whether oil production for federal wartime contracts decades ago allows removal to federal jurisdiction. Chief Justice Roberts and other justices questioned how far this precedent could extend, with potential implications for countless state court cases against corporations with any historical federal contracts.

GAO Report: Four of Five Federal Programs Lack Basic Fraud Prevention Processes

Read more
A GAO audit published January 5 found that four out of five federal programs examined failed to document key fraud prevention processes, contrary to legal requirements and leading practices. The programs award billions annually but lack systematic fraud risk assessments. The report highlights a gap between anti-fraud mandates and actual implementation across agencies. This comes as federal oversight mechanisms face significant disruption, with multiple inspector general offices losing substantial staff in 2025.

FBI Terrorist Watchlist Alerts Confuse Local Police in Half of Interviews

Read more
A January 12 GAO report found that in half of interviews with law enforcement agencies, officers said they may not know how to properly respond to alerts when state database systems flag a name matching the FBI's terrorist watchlist during routine traffic stops. The alerts appear in state systems but often lack clear guidance on appropriate action. The finding raises questions about both civil liberties and officer safety during routine encounters where watchlist matches may be false positives or lack actionable context.

Trump Administration Faces First Major Supreme Court Defeat on National Guard Federalization

Read more
In a December 23 ruling, the Supreme Court voted 6-3 to block President Trump's attempt to federalize Illinois National Guard forces, finding he likely lacked authority under the statute invoked. Legal analysts note the decision's reasoning creates new obstacles for two alternative paths: deploying active-duty forces under inherent constitutional authority or invoking the Insurrection Act. The majority held that federalization is only permitted when active-duty forces would be insufficient—a standard the administration did not meet. Trump withdrew similar deployments in Los Angeles and Portland following the ruling but promised to return "in a much different and stronger form."
