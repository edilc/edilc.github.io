Supreme Court Rules Victim Restitution Under 1996 Act is Criminal Punishment, Overturning Decades of Lower Court Decisions

In a 9-0 decision authored by Justice Kavanaugh on January 20, 2026, the Supreme Court held that restitution under the Mandatory Victims Restitution Act is criminal punishment subject to Ex Post Facto Clause protections. The ruling reversed the Eighth Circuit and resolved a circuit split, establishing that defendants cannot be ordered to pay restitution for crimes committed before the 1996 law took effect. The decision scrutinized the Act's text, noting it labels restitution as a 'penalty' and permits imprisonment for non-payment. This unanimous ruling forces reexamination of thousands of restitution orders issued retroactively over nearly three decades.

Rare Disease

FDA Approves First Treatment for Menkes Disease, Ultra-Rare Copper Metabolism Disorder

On January 12, 2026, the FDA approved Zycubo (copper histidinate), a copper replacement therapy for Menkes disease in pediatric patients. Menkes disease is an X-linked recessive disorder affecting copper metabolism that typically causes death in early childhood from severe neurological degeneration. The approval represents the first specific treatment for a condition that affects approximately 1 in 100,000 births. The disease has been essentially untreatable since its description in 1962; affected infants typically die by age three. Early copper supplementation may prevent or slow neurological damage, making this approval a breakthrough for an ultra-rare pediatric disease.

PTAB Doubles Its Reversal Rate of Section 101 Patent Rejections Under New Director

Since Director John Squires took office, the Patent Trial and Appeal Board has doubled its rate of reversing Section 101 patent eligibility rejections, according to analysis of 2024-2025 ex parte appeals. The reversal rate jumped from 8-12% in early 2024 to 18% in October 2025 and spiked to 29% in November 2025. A key shift: the Board now demands evidentiary support for findings that claimed elements are 'well-understood, routine, conventional activity' under Alice step two, rather than accepting examiner assertions. This represents a significant pro-patent shift that could reshape software and business method patent prosecution without any change in law.

Nature Cell Biology: Asymmetric T Cell Division Requires Autophagy to Determine Immune Memory Fate

Published January 6, 2026 in Nature Cell Biology, Borsa et al. demonstrate that asymmetric T cell division after activation requires autophagy to promote mitochondrial turnover. The study shows that T cells inheriting older mitochondria display decreased degradation capacity, reduced memory potential, and altered metabolism. This reveals a mechanistic link between organelle age inheritance and immune cell fate—suggesting that cellular 'housekeeping' machinery directly determines whether activated T cells become long-lived memory cells or short-lived effector cells. The finding has implications for vaccine design and understanding why immune memory varies between individuals.
