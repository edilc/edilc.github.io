Federal Courts Overwhelmed by Immigration Detention Bond Cases
The Western District of Texas saw more habeas corpus filings in January 2026 than it had seen in any entire previous year, according to federal judge David Briones. This surge stems from Board of Immigration Appeals rulings that ended bond releases in immigration cases. Over 300 federal judges have ruled against the Trump administration on the issue (versus 14 in favor), with hundreds of detained immigrants successfully obtaining bonds after judges found the policy violates due process. The Fifth Circuit will hear arguments on February 11, 2026, potentially heading to the Supreme Court.

Chemical Recycling Breakthrough Separates Cotton and Polyester

Researchers at Avantium and University of Amsterdam developed a process using highly concentrated hydrochloric acid (43 wt%) at room temperature to separate mixed cotton-polyester textiles. The process achieved 75% cotton recovery as glucose and 78% polyester monomer recovery. Technology moves to demonstration plant in 2026, targeting 100,000 tons annually by decade's end.

Tandem Solar Cells Reach 34% Efficiency

Tandem perovskite solar cells combining perovskite and silicon have achieved power conversion efficiencies over 34%, compared to about 24% for existing commercial silicon panels. Improvements stem from breakthroughs in interface passivation and compositional tuning with rubidium and cesium. The technology represents significant progress in both efficiency and stability.

NEJM Publishes Independent Vaccine Evidence Review
A systematic review of 17,263 references examined respiratory virus vaccines for the 2025-2026 season, published in NEJM. The independent review by academic researchers was conducted over 12 weeks after changes to federal advisory processes disrupted CDC guidance. Of 511 studies meeting inclusion criteria, findings reaffirmed immunizations reduce severe outcomes with infrequent safety events. The review explicitly notes it was necessary because "changes in the vaccine advisory process in the United States have disrupted immunization guidance."
